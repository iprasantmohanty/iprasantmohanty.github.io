About me

A Brief Introduction

I am a faculty member in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at SRM University AP, Andhra Pradesh, India. I have recently submitted my Ph.D. thesis in Computer Science & Engineering at the National Institute of Technology Meghalaya (NIT Meghalaya), India.

Prior to this, I have gained more than ten years of teaching experience at various reputed engineering institutes. My research interests lie in the broad area of trustworthy machine learning, with a focus on enhancing interpretability, fairness, privacy, and explainability of machine learning models, including generative and other pre-trained models. I have published several research articles in reputed international journals and conferences.

My research interests lie within the broad area of trustworthy machine learning. More specifically, I focus on improving the interpretability, fairness, privacy, and explanable capabilities of different kinds of ML models, including generative models and other pre-trained models. My research addresses the following fundamental questions pertaining to human and algorithmic decision-making:

  1. How can we build interpretable and accurate models to personalize health recomendations in human decision-making?
  2. How do we train and evaluate models when faced with missing counterfactuals?

These questions have far-reaching implications in domains involving high-stakes decisions such as health care, policy, law, and business.
